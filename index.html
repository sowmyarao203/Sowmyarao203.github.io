Conversation Engine for Dumb and Deaf

We are currently utilizing convolutional neural networks (CNNs) to develop a model that can accurately recognize and interpret various hand gestures. This model is trained on a diverse set of gestures commonly used by individuals who are deaf and dumb. To make this technology accessible and practical, we have created a user-friendly mobile application that integrates the trained model. This app serves as a communication tool, allowing deaf and dumb individuals to convey their messages through sign language. The app seamlessly translates these signs into spoken language, enabling normal users to understand and respond accordingly. This innovation aims to bridge the communication gap and empower deaf and dumb individuals to effectively interact with others in a meaningful and inclusive manner.
